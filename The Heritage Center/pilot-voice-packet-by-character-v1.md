# The Heritage Center — Pilot Voice Packet by Character v1
## Episode 1: Accredited Ancestors

## Purpose
This file reorganizes the interview-driven pilot into a character-by-character audio packet so recording or generating voices is easier, faster, and more organic.

Use this after reviewing:
- `pilot-script-draft-v2.md`
- `pilot-timed-production-draft-v1.md`

This packet is built to help the user:
- record by character
- revise line delivery more naturally
- trim or expand per speaker
- avoid the pilot sounding fully AI-generated

---

## GENERAL PERFORMANCE RULES
- Do not perform every line like polished narration.
- Let different characters have different levels of precision.
- Keep tiny hesitations, false starts, and emphasis shifts when useful.
- Classroom lines should feel reactive.
- Interview lines should feel like people rationalizing themselves in private.

---

# A1
## Voice profile
- clipped
- self-serious
- trying to sound official
- frustrated that nobody else respects structure
- should sound like a man attempting to bureaucratize disorder

## Classroom lines
1. If we could just cross-reference for forty-five minutes, we can leave here with a mission statement, a cleaned curriculum outline, and donor-facing language that does not immediately sound insane.
2. Question one. In one sentence, how do we define the Heritage Center as an institution?
3. None of that works.
4. Someone has to think like him. That is what accreditation is.
5. The Genealogy Conference has to sound real enough for donors and clean enough for accreditation review.
6. It needs to sound real to people who wear blazers.
7. What is a bloodline package?
8. What exactly are we promising the students?
9. Can nobody in this room answer a question directly?

## Interview lines
1. People think accreditation is about truth. It is not. It is about order. Presentation. Structure. Consistency. Language.
2. If the packet is clean, they are forced to respect the vision.
3. Now whether the vision is technically finished — that’s not the same conversation.
4. This is the problem with Rootwords. He thinks audience creation and institutional construction are interchangeable.
5. They are not interchangeable until the paperwork is in order.
6. The students need sequence. They need modules. They need a curriculum that can survive contact with daylight.
7. You cannot keep calling every emotional breakthrough a course.
8. Chief Holly Bey thinks legacy is leadership. Dense El thinks volume is leadership. Rootwords thinks reach is leadership. Mild thinks possession is leadership. Olmec thinks distance from specificity is leadership.
9. And then they wonder why I’m the only one building binders.
10. Directness is very underrated in communities that claim to honor truth.
11. If I can get them to stop performing long enough to standardize the language, this place can still become legitimate.

## Delivery notes
- Keep his sentences more controlled than the others.
- Let irritation leak in only at the ends of lines.
- He should sound like he believes order is morality.

# CHIEF HOLLY BEY
## Voice profile
- calm
- declarative
- founder certainty
- speaks like his authority predates procedure
- should sound legacy-minded and mildly offended by administration

## Classroom lines
1. We are a sovereign academic restoration body.
2. That’s because you keep thinking like a pale face.
3. No. Convocation.

## Interview lines
1. A1 thinks institutions are built by approval. That’s because A1 still thinks rejection is proof you should become cleaner.
2. Rejection is usually proof you’re saying something first.
3. A1 uses the word order the way weak nations use the word peace.
4. The students do not come here for modules. They come here because something in them already knows the official story is sick.
5. Our job is not to make them comfortable. Our job is to wake up what they already suspect.
6. A1 is the kind of man who would laminate a prophecy.
7. Directness is overrated by people who think authority comes from neatness.
8. If this place ever becomes too legitimate, it will probably stop being useful.

## Delivery notes
- Never rush him.
- He should sound composed even when insulting someone.
- Lines should land like founder doctrine, not jokes.

## Interview lines

6. Dense El think everything is a showdown. A1 think everything is a packet. Holly think everything is legacy. Olmec think everything is cosmic. Mild think everything is in the bag somewhere.
7. And all of them need me more than they admit.
8. Honestly this was the healthiest the room looked all day.

## Delivery notes
- Let him sound lighter than everybody else.
- He should sound like he is discovering the joke while saying it.
- Not lazy — fast and opportunistic.

# HUMANIZING NOTES
To make the final audio feel less fully AI-generated:
- let some takes run slightly long
- keep a few imperfect breaths
- do not over-clean every pause
- allow A1 and Dense El to overlap more sharply in classroom material
- let Mild Indian search for words
- let Rootwords sound amused by his own logic
- let Holly Bey sound like he is never being interviewed by an equal
- let Olmec sound like he is translating from a different discipline entirely
