# The Heritage Center — Pilot Voice Packet Minimum Viable v1
## Episode 1: Accredited Ancestors

## Purpose
This is the stripped-down audio packet for the fastest possible usable pilot cut.

Use this when you do **not** want to record every available line first.
The goal here is to get a rough pilot assembled with only the dialogue absolutely needed to make the concept work.

This packet is built to support:
- a still-based pilot
- classroom + interview room only
- a first cut that can later be expanded with alternate takes or extra lines

---

## MVP PRINCIPLE
Record only the lines that do one of these jobs:
1. establish the room
2. establish A1’s problem
3. establish the founder / disruptor / document guy / promo guy / mystic roles
4. explain what the institution actually is
5. show what they think about students
6. show what they think about each other
7. end on a strong contradiction

Everything else can be added later.

---

## RECOMMENDED MVP RUNTIME
- **Classroom:** 4:30 to 6:00
- **Interviews:** 4:00 to 5:30
- **Total rough cut:** about 9 to 11.5 minutes

This is enough to prove the pilot works without over-recording.

# INTERVIEW LINES — ABSOLUTE MINIMUM
These are the minimum confessionals needed to make the pilot feel complete.

## A1
1. People think accreditation is about truth. It is not. It is about order. Presentation. Structure. Consistency. Language.
2. The students need sequence. They need modules. They need a curriculum that can survive contact with daylight.
3. Chief Holly Bey thinks legacy is leadership. Dense El thinks volume is leadership. Rootwords thinks reach is leadership. Mild thinks possession is leadership. Olmec thinks distance from specificity is leadership.
4. If I can get them to stop performing long enough to standardize the language, this place can still become legitimate.

## Chief Holly Bey
1. A1 thinks institutions are built by approval. That’s because A1 still thinks rejection is proof you should become cleaner.
2. The students do not come here for modules. They come here because something in them already knows the official story is sick.
3. A1 is the kind of man who would laminate a prophecy.
4. If this place ever becomes too legitimate, it will probably stop being useful.

## Mr. Dense El
1. The problem with this place is not intelligence. It’s tone.
2. Students say they want information. What they really want is confidence.
3. And I’m the only one in here who can actually hold a crowd.
4. A room don’t need peace to have power.

## Rootwords
1. Nobody under forty want a pathway. They want a clip.
2. People keep acting like promotion is corruption. It’s not corruption if the thing exists.
3. If a student can’t explain what you taught in a clip, you probably taught it like a hostage note.
4. Honestly? If we cut the right trailer out of today, the conference might sell out.

## Mild Indian
1. I’m not disorganized. I’m just working across too many channels of truth at one time.
2. The problem with this Center is nobody respects raw evidence.
3. Students appreciate depth. The issue is they be wanting depth pre-sorted.
4. I still think I had the right screenshot earlier.

## Olmec Donald
1. Everybody in that room believes in lineage. They just disagree about the form in which lineage arrives.
2. Documents are only one form of remembrance. People worship paper because paper flatters control.
3. Every institution eventually reveals what each man is using it for.
4. Most institutions die from clarity. This one may survive confusion.
