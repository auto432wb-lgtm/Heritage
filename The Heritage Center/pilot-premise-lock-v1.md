# The Heritage Center — Pilot Premise Lock v1

## Episode 1 Working Title
**Accredited Ancestors**

## Pilot Premise
The Heritage Center is in the middle of an urgent effort to regain accreditation while also trying to raise money for an upcoming Genealogy Conference that they believe will restore their status, expand their influence, and prove they are a legitimate educational institution.

The problem: everyone involved has their own niche, ego, audience, and theory of what the Center should be — and nobody is capable of behaving like an actual school.

## Core Episode Engine
The staff are forced to perform credibility, unity, and institutional seriousness under pressure, even though every member of the organization is driven by personal branding, pseudo-scholarship, niche obsessions, and internal status competition.

## Character Lock Correction
A1 is not the sane one.

He is:
- pseudo in a cleaner outfit
- addicted to legitimacy
- obsessed with accreditation language
- desperate for official recognition
- frustrated that everyone else is too sloppy to help him institutionalize the nonsense properly

A1’s core logic:
**If the lies are organized correctly, they become curriculum.**

## A-Story
A1 is trying to organize the Heritage Center’s accreditation recovery effort:
- institutional packet
- language cleanup
- curriculum presentation
- public image
- donor-facing professionalism

He believes if he can make the Center look credible on paper, they can regain standing.

## B-Story
The rest of the Center is trying to raise money and attention for the upcoming Genealogy Conference, which they see as:
- a fundraiser
- a prestige event
- a recruitment engine
- a public validation moment
- a chance to expand their own niche and status

Everyone wants control over the conference because each person sees it as a route to relevance.

## Pilot Conflict
The accreditation push and the fundraising push are supposed to help each other.

Instead, they make each other worse.
- accreditation requires discipline
- fundraising requires performance
- performance increases chaos
- chaos ruins credibility
- failed credibility makes everyone overcompensate
- overcompensation creates more chaos

## Cold Open Lock
The episode opens mid-meeting with total chaos.

The Heritage Center staff are trying to finalize:
- accreditation documents
- conference fundraising language
- speaker billing
- the official mission statement
- the genealogy evidence room
- donor outreach

Nobody agrees on:
- what the Center teaches
- what accreditation board they are even targeting
- what genealogy means
- who gets keynote placement
- whether evidence is necessary
- whether the conference should feel academic, spiritual, revolutionary, or viral

A1 tries to run the meeting like a real institution. It immediately devolves.

## Episode Story Shape
### Movement 1
- cold open chaos meeting
- mockumentary interviews establish each person’s self-image
- A1 explains the urgency of regaining accreditation
- fundraising pressure for the Genealogy Conference becomes clear
- everyone claims to be helping

### Movement 2
- attempts to professionalize the Center begin
- conference materials are rewritten, argued over, and inflated
- Mild Indian’s documents become central despite being unusable
- Mr. Dense El makes every planning space about dominance
- Rootwords keeps turning everything into content
- Olmec Donald introduces mystical framing that derails practical planning
- Chief Holly Bey insists true institutions always reject prophets at first

### Movement 3
- they try to film, pitch, rehearse, or present themselves in a more official way
- internal contradictions become impossible to hide
- the donor/accreditation/fundraising effort partially implodes
- someone accidentally generates momentum through the same unserious behavior that caused the collapse

## Ending Beat Lock
They do not become credible.

But they do gain just enough:
- money
- attention
- false hope
- donor confusion
- social momentum

to keep the Center alive for the next episode.

The pilot should end with continuing dysfunction, not closure.

## Main Comic Contradictions
### Chief Holly Bey
**Public:** “I founded this Center to restore truth.”

**Interview:** “If they can’t cite me, they shouldn’t be teaching here.”

**Contradiction:** He cares as much about ownership and legacy as truth.

### Mr. Dense El
**Public:** “I’m here to educate with passion.”

**Interview:** “Some people only understand correction when it’s public.”

**Contradiction:** He uses teaching to dominate.

### Mild Indian
**Public:** “I brought the documents.”

**Interview:** “My phone died, but the evidence is in the energy of the screenshots.”

**Contradiction:** He has endless material and no usable proof.

### Olmec Donald
**Public:** “History must be felt as well as studied.”

**Interview:** “Paper is colonial. Vibration is original.”

**Contradiction:** He escapes precision every time he is challenged.

### Rootwords
**Public:** “I’m one of the next great teachers.”

**Interview:** “I’ve really only been studying for like... intensely... for three months.”

**Contradiction:** He has maximum certainty and minimum foundation.

### A1
**Public:** “We are building a serious educational institution.”

**Interview:** “If the packet is clean enough, they don’t need to understand everything immediately.”

**Contradiction:** He mistakes formal presentation for legitimacy.

## One-Sentence Pilot Lock
While scrambling to regain accreditation, the staff of The Heritage Center try to raise money for a Genealogy Conference, only to undermine their own credibility through ego, niche pseudo-scholarship, and institutional chaos.
