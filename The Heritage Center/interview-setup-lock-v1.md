# The Heritage Center — Interview Setup Lock v1

## Purpose
Lock the mockumentary confessional format so every interview feels like it belongs to the same series.

## Core Interview Concept
The Heritage Center interviews should feel like the Center or its documentary partners tried to create a professional institutional interview setup, but the result is only semi-credible.

It should feel:
- official on purpose
- slightly cheap in execution
- consistent from character to character
- serious enough to support deadpan comedy

---

## Base Setup
### Framing
- medium seated interview
- chest-up or waist-up depending on character energy
- slight asymmetry is okay
- camera should feel documentary, not cinematic glamour

### Background
- Heritage Center banner, sign, or institutional backdrop
- enough visible detail to remind us this is a school-like environment
- should not be visually busy

### Lighting
- soft but slightly imperfect
- trying to look official, not beautiful
- enough separation to keep characters readable
- allow mild inconsistency that feels real to a low-budget institutional setup

### Camera feel
- observational documentary
- no flashy lensing
- static or nearly static
- subtle push-in only if absolutely needed later in edit

---

## Tone Rule
The interview setup should allow the character’s own delusion, ego, and contradiction to carry the joke.

The frame should not be more absurd than the person.

---

## Character-Specific Adjustments
### A1
- tidier seating posture
- slightly cleaner background angle if desired
- should look like he requested the “official” interview corner

### Chief Holly Bey
- posture should feel ceremonial / founder-like
- framing can feel a little more stately without changing the basic setup

### Mr. Dense El
- can lean forward or occupy frame aggressively
- setup should tolerate bigger body language

### Mild Indian
- same setup, but his appearance and posture create the instability
- slightly wrinkled or disorganized personal presentation can do the work

### Olmec Donald
- same setup, but wardrobe and composure give the frame a sacred-poet feel

### Rootwords
- slightly less settled posture
- should feel like someone thrilled to have been given the interview chair

---

## Must-Have Interview Props / Background Elements
- Heritage Center banner or logo
- chair or stool used consistently
- visible institutional wall or backdrop
- optional side table / folder / paper stack if needed

Avoid overloading the frame with books, maps, props, or mystical objects. The humor should come from what is said.

---

## What to Generate
1. core interview master setup
2. A1 interview variant if needed
3. all six character interview references in same framing family

---

## Editing Rule
Interviews should be reusable across multiple episodes and should cut cleanly against:
- classroom scenes
- livestream scenes
- office scenes
- archive scenes

This means consistency matters more than visual ambition.
