## Global Style Lock
Apply this language to most prompts unless a shot specifically needs something else.

**Style baseline:**
mockumentary realism, grounded comedy, documentary camera language, natural indoor lighting, slightly cheap institutional environment, real faces, believable bodies, imperfect but cinematic framing, local educational center aesthetics, no glossy commercial polish, no fantasy stylization, no hyper-dramatic blockbuster lighting

**Production language to reuse:**
- realistic documentary coverage
- handheld or observational camera feeling
- grounded ensemble comedy
- institutional clutter
- natural facial detail
- practical wardrobe
- consistent classroom continuity
- same characters as locked interview canon

**Negative steering baseline:**
avoid duplicated people, avoid extra fingers, avoid warped hands, avoid unreadable background text, avoid fashion-editorial glamour, avoid over-stylized cinematic extremes, avoid perfect luxury interiors, avoid cartoonish satire, avoid plastic skin, avoid exaggerated expressions unless specified

### Classroom continuity notes
Keep consistent across classroom prompts:

- binders and loose papers
- cheap chairs

### Office continuity notes
Keep consistent across office prompts:
- cramped office
- stacked binders
- desk with folders and papers
- printer-paper bureaucracy feel
- “ACCREDITATION REENTRY” vibe

# PRACTICAL USAGE METHOD
For each prompt batch:
1. keep room continuity fixed
2. keep wardrobe continuity fixed
3. use the locked interview stills as character identity anchors
4. generate 3–6 variations per important shot
5. choose one visual canon version before moving to the next beat
6. do not jump randomly across the episode if you have not locked the room logic

Recommended order:
- cold open
- conference fight
- document spill
- donor pitch
- rehearsal breakdown
- live meltdown
- office aftermath
- stinger
