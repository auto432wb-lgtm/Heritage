# The Heritage Center — Pilot Timed Production Draft v1
## Episode 1: Accredited Ancestors

## Purpose
This file translates the interview-driven pilot into a practical runtime structure so audio can be recorded, trimmed, and revised with intention.

Target use:
- keep classroom dialogue to roughly 5–8 minutes
- keep interview/confessional material to roughly 6 minutes
- let the user adjust the final character audio packet to feel more organic and less fully AI-generated

This is not a final locked runtime.
It is a working timing draft for pilot assembly.

---

## Target Episode Runtime
### Flexible target:
**11 to 14 minutes total**

### Preferred target:
**12 to 13 minutes total**

### Target split:
- **Classroom / observational material:** 5:30 to 7:00
- **Interview / confessional material:** 5:00 to 6:00
- **Title / pauses / transitions / holds:** 0:30 to 1:00

---

## Editorial Principle
The classroom should not carry the burden of explaining the whole show.
It should:
- trigger conflict
- establish power
- create visual comedy
- reveal interpersonal friction
- give the interviews something to reinterpret

The interview room should do the deeper work:
- motives
- philosophy
- ego
- contradictions
- how they feel about students
- how they feel about the information
- how they feel about each other

# TIMED STRUCTURE

## 0. OPENING CLASSROOM SETUP
**Target runtime:** 1:20–1:45  
**Mode:** classroom

### Purpose
- establish room
- establish accreditation pressure
- establish ensemble dysfunction
- launch the episode cleanly

### Suggested content
- CL-01 classroom master
- CL-02 A1 leads the meeting
- CL-03 group reaction baseline
- short overlap from Holly Bey / Dense El / Mild / Rootwords / Olmec
- classroom silence after A1 says the pale-face line

Keep this sharp. Don’t overplay every line.

### Timing note
If this goes past 1:45, cut it down.
The pilot should get to confessional logic early.

# AUDIO WORKFLOW RECOMMENDATION

## Step 1 — record classroom first
Why:
- it gives you the backbone rhythm
- it tells you how much interview material you actually need
- it prevents over-recording interviews that the edit won’t support

### Record classroom in these chunks
1. opening meeting chaos
2. conference language fight
3. student question / stall
4. breakdown section
5. final Mild Indian button

---

## Step 2 — record interviews second
Why:
- once classroom timing exists, you can make interview answers feel responsive
- you can trim interview monologues to fit the edit instead of guessing blind

### Record interviews in these thematic packets
1. what this room really is
2. information / promotion / documents
3. what they think of students
4. what they think of each other
5. final one-line buttons

---

## Step 3 — humanize the audio packet
To keep it from feeling fully AI-generated:
- vary line lengths
- let some characters ramble and self-correct
- leave in tiny hesitations
- give different characters different cadence discipline
- avoid making every interview answer equally polished

### Character rhythm notes
- **A1:** clipped, structured, trying to sound official even when panicked
- **Holly Bey:** calm, declarative, founder certainty
- **Dense El:** forceful, confrontational, performance-ready
- **Rootwords:** nimble, casual, media-brained, amused by rigidity
- **Mild Indian:** overloaded, searching, sincere, slightly unstable flow
- **Olmec Donald:** smooth, slow, reflective, abstract confidence

---

# TRIM RULES
If the pilot runs long, cut in this order:
1. shorten interview answers before cutting classroom triggers
2. shorten repeated philosophy lines before cutting unique character contradictions
3. reduce any interview block that restates the same point twice
4. keep the final classroom button no matter what

---

# FINAL NOTE
This pilot should feel like a room that cannot define itself,
followed by six men each explaining why they are the only one who understands what the room really means.

If the timing supports that, the pilot works.
