# The Heritage Center — Premise & Engine Lock v1

## Core Premise
The Heritage Center is a mockumentary-style comedy series set inside a chaotic pseudo-academic institution run by self-appointed online historians, livestream intellectuals, and identity entrepreneurs who are trying to turn their misinformation ecosystem into a legitimate school.

They present themselves as scholars, teachers, and truth-preservers.

In reality, they are:
- competitive
- insecure
- branding themselves constantly
- fighting for internal status
- obsessed with being right without doing real research
- confusing charisma, screenshots, vibes, and ego with evidence

The comedy comes from the gap between:
- the seriousness with which they present themselves
and
- the absurdity, pettiness, and instability of the institution they have built.

## Series Engine
A group of deeply unserious but highly confident pseudo-intellectuals are trying to make The Heritage Center into a legitimate educational institution and media platform, while constantly undermining themselves through ego, misinformation, personal conflict, bad scholarship, and livestream-era clout behavior.

This gives the show repeatable story engines through:
- attempts at legitimacy
- internal power struggles
- fake scholarship
- public-facing branding
- petty personal rivalries
- exposure risk
- chaotic students, guests, donors, and critics
- the contradiction between what the school claims to be and what it actually is

## Mini-Series / Season Arc
The Heritage Center is trying to launch itself as a real academy, streaming school, and cultural institution before it collapses under the weight of its own incompetence, internal ego wars, and inability to survive real scrutiny.

The larger season can be driven by:
- regaining accreditation
- hosting a major conference
- launching an online university
- attracting donors or paying members
- surviving internal faction fights
- avoiding exposure by real historians
- stopping the building or platform from falling apart
- keeping students and supporters from realizing nobody truly knows what they are talking about

## Episode Structure Lock
Each episode generally runs on this rhythm:
1. Cold open
2. Classroom, livestream, or community-center scene
3. Solo interview confessionals
4. Contradiction montage
5. Escalating absurdity
6. Emotional or petty personal conflict underneath the scholarship
7. Stinger joke

## Key Comic Mechanic
Everyone behaves as if they are preserving buried truth, but they are actually building identity, clout, and status through bad evidence, emotional reasoning, and pseudo-academic performance.

They are not just wrong.

They are:
- wrong with confidence
- wrong competitively
- wrong institutionally
- wrong on camera
- wrong while trying to become official

## Interview Structure Lock
Each major character should always operate on three levels:
1. Public self-image
2. Private confessional identity
3. Visible contradiction

### Example dynamic
**Public:**
“I am preserving the truth they buried.”

**Interview:**
“I mean, technically I haven’t finished the source material. But spiritually, I already know what it says.”

**Visible contradiction:**
They are bluffing, stalling, improvising, and trying to save face.

## Visual Production Lock
Keep the visual world manageable.

Primary recurring spaces:
- one classroom
- one office / principal room
- one livestream studio
- one hallway / community-center common area
- one archive / fake records room
- optional exterior entrance / parking lot

The comedy should come primarily from:
- performance
- blocking
- interviews
- props
- recurring visual details
- the contradiction between institutional presentation and actual dysfunction

## Tone Lock
The tone should be:
- mockumentary
- ensemble comedy
- deadpan but absurd
- character-driven
- satirical
- petty, insecure, and status-obsessed underneath fake intellectual seriousness

It should feel like:
- a school
- a cult of personality
- a livestream brand house
- and a failing institution
all at once.

## One-Sentence Lock
The Heritage Center is a mockumentary comedy about a group of self-important pseudo-historians trying to turn their livestream misinformation ecosystem into a legitimate school, while ego, bad scholarship, and status warfare make legitimacy impossible.
