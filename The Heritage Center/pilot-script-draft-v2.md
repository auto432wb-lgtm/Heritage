# The Heritage Center — Pilot Script Draft v2
## Episode 1: Accredited Ancestors

## Revised Production Intent
This version is built for a still-based mockumentary pilot where the classroom provides the observational spine, but the interview room carries most of the real dialogue, explanation, contradiction, and character psychology.

The classroom scenes should now function more like:
- visual setup
- conflict triggers
- reaction tableaux
- overlapping fragments
- moments that send characters into confessional mode

The interview room should now function as:
- the primary dialogue engine
- the place where each teacher reveals what they really think
- the place where they reinterpret what just happened in the classroom
- the place where attitudes toward information, students, legitimacy, and each other are fully expressed

This is not a standard scene-heavy sitcom structure anymore.
It is closer to a mockumentary collage built from:
- classroom incidents
- confessional monologues
- contradiction cuts
- reaction-based escalation

### COLD OPEN

**INT. HERITAGE CENTER — MAIN CLASSROOM — DAY**

A folding table has been pushed to the front of a badly organized classroom.

A laminated sign taped to the wall reads:

**THE HERITAGE CENTER**  
**ACCREDITATION REENTRY TASK FORCE**

Underneath, handwritten in red marker:

**GENEALOGY CONFERENCE FUNDRAIZER GOAL: $12,000**

The room is already in motion.

Papers everywhere. Dry-erase maps. Half-dead laptops. Binders. A cracked podium. Flyers. Loose cables. A donation jar labeled **RECLAIM THE RECORD**.

A1 stands at the front with a folder, trying to project institutional authority.

**A1**  
If we could just cross-reference for forty-five minutes, we can leave here with a mission statement, a cleaned curriculum outline, and donor-facing language that does not immediately sound insane.

A beat.

Nobody receives this like faculty.

Chief Holly Bey leans back, already resistant.  
Dense El leans in like he’s seconds away from correcting somebody publicly.  
Rootwords is half-filming, half-participating.  
Mild Indian is buried in papers and one dead device.  
Olmec Donald writes something abstract on the whiteboard.

**A1**  
Question one. In one sentence, how do we define the Heritage Center as an institution?

Immediate overlap.

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY**  
We are a sovereign academic restoration body.

**MR. DENSE EL**  
No, no — we are a corrective intellectual tether taskforce.

**ROOTWORDS**  
We a internet cult.

**MILD INDIAN**  
Technically we teach genealogy.

**OLMEC DONALD**  
We are a remembering mechanism.

**A1**  
None of that works.

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY**  
That’s because you keep thinking like a pale face.

**A1**  
Someone has to think like him. That is what accreditation is.

Silence.

Everyone stares at him.

### INTERVIEW — A1

He sits in a plastic chair in front of a badly hung Heritage Center banner.

**A1**  
People think accreditation is about truth. It is not.  
It is about order.  
Presentation.  
Structure.  
Consistency.  
Language.

A beat.

**A1 (CONT'D)**  
If the packet is clean, they are forced to respect the vision.

Another beat.

**A1 (CONT'D)**  
Now whether the vision is technically finished — that’s not the same conversation.

### INTERVIEW — CHIEF HOLLY BEY

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY**  
A1 thinks institutions are built by approval.  
That’s because A1 still thinks rejection is proof you should become cleaner.

He leans forward.

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY (CONT'D)**  
Rejection is usually proof you’re saying something first.

### INTERVIEW — MR. DENSE EL

**MR. DENSE EL**  
The problem with this place is not intelligence.  
It’s tone.

A beat.

**MR. DENSE EL (CONT'D)**  
Too many people in here want to sound acceptable before they sound true.

### INTERVIEW — ROOTWORDS

**ROOTWORDS**  
You can tell when a room is losing the youth because everybody starts saying words like institution and framework and pathway.

He shrugs.

**ROOTWORDS (CONT'D)**  
Nobody under forty want a pathway. They want a clip.

### INTERVIEW — MILD INDIAN

**MILD INDIAN**  
I’m not disorganized.  
I’m just working across too many channels of truth at one time.

He looks down.

**MILD INDIAN (CONT'D)**  
Also my phone was dead.

### INTERVIEW — OLMEC DONALD

**OLMEC DONALD**  
Everybody in that room believes in lineage.  
They just disagree about the form in which lineage arrives.

A beat.

**OLMEC DONALD (CONT'D)**  
Some people need a document.  
Some need a title.  
Some need applause.  
Some need vibration.

### BACK TO CLASSROOM

Mild Indian quietly lifts a cracked tablet.

**MILD INDIAN**  
I found one of the screenshots.

The screen is black.

**MILD INDIAN (CONT'D)**  
It was there earlier.

### TITLE CARD
**THE HERITAGE CENTER**

## STRUCTURAL NOTE
From this point forward, classroom scenes should be brief and functional.
They trigger the interviews.
The interviews do the real narrative lifting.

Use the classroom to show:
- who interrupted whom
- what visual absurdity just occurred
- who is annoyed
- who feels powerful
- what prop or paper or phone caused the next talking-head rant

Use the interview room to reveal:
- what they actually think about the information
- what they think of the students
- what they think of each other
- how they justify themselves
- how they reinterpret every incident in their own favor

---

### SEQUENCE 1 — WHAT THE CENTER IS SUPPOSED TO BE

**INT. MAIN CLASSROOM — LATER**

A1 is at the whiteboard trying to shape the Genealogy Conference into something that sounds fundable and accreditable.

On the whiteboard:
- Bloodline Verification
- Maritime Origins
- Burial Records
- Colonial Name Conversion
- Hidden Nobility
- Sacred Cartography

Rootwords is already on his phone.  
Dense El looks bored by moderation.  
Holly Bey is ready to overrule the language.  
Mild Indian has too many papers.  
Olmec looks like he is waiting for the room to become symbolic.

**A1**  
The Genealogy Conference has to sound real enough for donors and clean enough for accreditation review.

**MR. DENSE EL**  
It is real.

**A1**  
It needs to sound real to people who wear blazers.

**ROOTWORDS**  
Then say symposium.

**MR. DENSE EL**  
No. Summit.

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY**  
No. Convocation.

**MILD INDIAN**  
I thought it was a lineage.

Everyone turns.

**MILD INDIAN (CONT'D)**  
That’s what the flyer says.

### INTERVIEW — ROOTWORDS

**ROOTWORDS**  
People keep acting like promotion is corruption.  
It’s not corruption if the thing exists.

A beat.

**ROOTWORDS (CONT'D)**  
And if the thing don’t fully exist yet, promotion can help summon it.

### INTERVIEW — A1

**A1**  
This is the problem with Rootwords.  
He thinks audience creation and institutional construction are interchangeable.

A beat.

**A1 (CONT'D)**  
They are not interchangeable until the paperwork is in order.

### INTERVIEW — CHIEF HOLLY BEY

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY**  
A1 uses the word order the way weak nations use the word peace.

**A1**

Premium access.

Premium access to what?

**MR. DENSE EL**  
Earlier knowledge.

**MILD INDIAN**

- maps

- handwritten arrows

The problem with this Center is nobody respects raw evidence.

A beat.

**MILD INDIAN (CONT'D)**

### INTERVIEW — MR. DENSE EL

A beat.

**MR. DENSE EL (CONT'D)**

### SEQUENCE 3 — WHAT THEY THINK OF STUDENTS

**INT. MAIN CLASSROOM — A LITTLE LATER**

A1 tries again to turn the room into something teachable.

**A1**  
What exactly are we promising the students?

This lands harder than expected.

Nobody answers immediately.

### INTERVIEW — A1

**A1**  
The students need sequence.  
They need modules.  
They need a curriculum that can survive contact with daylight.

He tightens his jaw.

**A1 (CONT'D)**  
You cannot keep calling every emotional breakthrough a course.

### INTERVIEW — CHIEF HOLLY BEY

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY**  
The students do not come here for modules.  
They come here because something in them already knows the official story is sick.

A beat.

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY (CONT'D)**  
Our job is not to make them comfortable.  
Our job is to wake up what they already suspect.

### INTERVIEW — MR. DENSE EL

**MR. DENSE EL**  
Students say they want information.  
What they really want is confidence.

He leans in.

**MR. DENSE EL (CONT'D)**  
Information without posture just create another scared researcher.

### INTERVIEW — ROOTWORDS

**ROOTWORDS**  
If a student can’t explain what you taught in a clip, you probably taught it like a hostage note.

### INTERVIEW — MILD INDIAN

**MILD INDIAN**

The issue is they be wanting depth pre-sorted.

### INTERVIEW — OLMEC DONALD

**OLMEC DONALD**  
Students are usually less afraid of mystery than teachers are.

### INTERVIEW — CHIEF HOLLY BEY

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY**

### INTERVIEW — MR. DENSE EL

**MR. DENSE EL**

### INTERVIEW — ROOTWORDS

**ROOTWORDS**

### INTERVIEW — MILD INDIAN

### ENDING SEQUENCE

**INT. MAIN CLASSROOM — END OF DAY**

The room is quieter now.

The papers are worse.  
The board is less useful.  
Nothing has been resolved.  
Nobody has agreed on language, structure, evidence, or pedagogy.

And yet the Center still feels alive.

A1 stands in the wreckage with his folder.

### INTERVIEW — A1

**A1**  
If I can get them to stop performing long enough to standardize the language, this place can still become legitimate.

### INTERVIEW — CHIEF HOLLY BEY

**CHIEF HOLLY BEY**  
If this place ever becomes too legitimate, it will probably stop being useful.

### INTERVIEW — MR. DENSE EL

**MR. DENSE EL**

### INTERVIEW — ROOTWORDS

**ROOTWORDS**

### INTERVIEW — MILD INDIAN

**MILD INDIAN**

**OLMEC DONALD**

This one may survive confusion.

Mild Indian, still searching through his stack, pulls out a receipt and looks at it like revelation.

**MILD INDIAN**

**MILD INDIAN (CONT'D)**
