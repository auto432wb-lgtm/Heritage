# The Heritage Center — Pilot 3-Room Generation Plan v1

## Goal
Adapt the Episode 1 pilot into a contained proof-of-concept that uses only three repeatable environments:
- classroom
- A1 office
- interview room

This version is built for fast AI-assisted production, stronger visual consistency, lower generation overhead, and easier assembly into a watchable pilot edit.

---

## Core Principle
Do not treat the limited room count as a downgrade.
Treat it as a stylistic choice.

The pilot should feel like:
- a contained mockumentary episode
- a small institution constantly folding in on itself
- a world where everything important happens in the same few spaces
- a pressure cooker of ego, pseudo-legitimacy, and documentary exposure

This means scenes originally assigned to hallway, archive, or livestream space should be absorbed into:
- classroom for ensemble conflict, donor pitch rehearsal, document chaos, and accidental public performance
- office for accreditation stress, pseudo-bureaucratic planning, and aftermath
- interview room for exposition, contradiction, and character-specific punchlines

---

## Room Functions

### 1) Classroom
**Use for:**
- cold open chaos
- all main ensemble scenes
- conference branding conflict
- donor pitch rehearsal
- accidental “live” style moments
- Mild Indian document reveals
- whiteboard arguments
- fake institutional activity

**Visual identity:**
- folding tables
- whiteboard with genealogy and accreditation language
- taped signage
- ring light or phone tripod in corner when needed
- cheap classroom chairs
- binders, papers, flyers, jars, chargers, cables, handouts

**Story role:**
The classroom is the Center’s public brain, war room, stage, and collapse zone.

---

### 2) A1 Office
**Use for:**
- A1 accreditation scenes
- command-center exposition
- mission statement revisions
- private fallout scenes
- strategic reframing after public chaos
- bureaucratic delusion and institutional self-justification

**Visual identity:**
- cramped office
- stacked binders
- desk lamp
- printer paper, folders, sticky notes
- “ACCREDITATION REENTRY” materials
- a cleaner visual language than the classroom, but still flimsy

**Story role:**
The office is where nonsense gets organized into policy language.

---

### 3) Interview Room
**Use for:**
- all confessionals
- character introductions
- contradiction reveals
- worldview explanations
- joke buttons after scenes
- exposition that would otherwise require extra locations

**Visual identity:**
- documentary camera setup
- simple chair
- branded Heritage Center banner or institutional backdrop
- soft interview lighting
- consistent framing across all characters

**Story role:**
The interview room is where everyone explains themselves badly.

## Scene 3 — Mild Indian and the Documents
**Original room:** archive / records room  
**New room:** classroom + interview room

**Adaptation:**
Move Mild Indian’s document chaos into the classroom.
He brings banker boxes, loose binders, screenshots, maps, and half-organized evidence directly into the meeting.
Instead of entering a separate archive world, the joke becomes that he has turned the classroom into an archive spill.

**How to stage it:**
- papers stacked beside chair
- maps sliding out of folders
- screenshots on cracked tablet or phone
- random receipts mixed with “evidence”
- interview confessional where he reframes disorganization as research abundance

**Why this works better for a 3-room pilot:**
It keeps Mild Indian integrated with the ensemble while preserving his identity as the unusable document guy.

**Needed generation:**
- Mild Indian with boxes/papers in classroom
- insert montage of fake evidence
- interview room confessional
- reaction shots from A1 and ensemble

---

## Scene 4 — Olmec Donald Mission Statement Conflict
**Original room:** hallway/common area  
**New room:** office + interview room

**Adaptation:**
Move the mission statement handoff into A1’s office.
Olmec enters the office with his revised spiritual version of the mission statement, contaminating the only room trying to remain professional.

**Why this works:**
The office is the cleanest “institutional” room, so Olmec’s mystical language lands harder there.

**Needed generation:**
- office two-shot: A1 + Olmec
- mission statement insert
- Olmec interview confessional
- A1 irritation reaction

---

## Scene 5 — Dense El Donor Pitch
**Original room:** livestream room  
**New room:** classroom corner staged as media zone + interview room

**Adaptation:**
Do not create a separate livestream room.
Instead, dedicate one corner of the classroom to a cheap media setup:
- ring light
- phone tripod
- crooked Heritage Center sign
- folding chair or desk
- donation QR/flyer nearby

Dense El rehearses or goes semi-live from this classroom media corner while others remain present in the room.

**Why this works:**
It keeps production simple and adds to the feeling that the Heritage Center uses the same room for everything: teaching, meetings, fundraising, and propaganda.

**Needed generation:**
- classroom media-corner master
- Dense El performance medium
- Rootwords watching/reacting
- A1 interruption shot
- Dense El interview confessional

## Scene 7 — The Fundraiser Goes Live Anyway
**Original room:** livestream room / classroom / hallway spillover  
**New room:** classroom only + interview room cutaways if needed

**Adaptation:**
The “live disaster” happens entirely in the classroom.
The media corner goes active while the rest of the room spills into frame.
This creates a stronger visual joke: the Center cannot separate performance, administration, and disorder.

**How to stage it:**
- ring light active in classroom
- phones up
- comments and donation overlays added in edit
- A1 forced into the same frame as everyone else
- Holly Bey drifting into the shot
- Olmec materializing into frame from off-angle
- Mild Indian holding up the wrong paper or map from inside the classroom clutter

**Why this works:**
The scene becomes more compact, more chaotic, and easier to generate.
It also makes the classroom feel like the beating heart of the pilot.

**Needed generation:**
- classroom live-event wide
- A1 panic reaction
- Dense El on-camera performance shot
- Holly Bey live interruption
- Olmec side insertion shot
- Mild Indian document gag
- donation/comment inserts for edit

## Editorial Strategy
Use the interview room aggressively.
It is not filler.
It is how the pilot gains scale without requiring extra locations.

The basic rhythm should be:
- classroom chaos
- cut to interview contradiction
- return to classroom escalation
- office scene for institutional framing
- back to classroom disorder
- interview cutaway to sharpen irony
- office aftermath

This creates a complete-feeling pilot from a very small production footprint.

---

## What to Cut or Compress
To keep the 3-room version clean, reduce or eliminate:
- separate hallway transitions
- separate archive geography
- separate livestream-room geography
- any beat that exists only to showcase a fourth location

If a joke or beat can happen in the classroom, keep it there.
If a contradiction can be explained in the interview room, use that instead of building another scene.
If A1 needs institutional pressure, move it to the office.

---

## Final Recommendation
Do not expand the pilot again.
The 3-room version is stronger for a first proof-of-concept because it:
- controls cost
- improves consistency
- reduces generation drift
- keeps the mockumentary style tight
- makes the episode more producible

The goal is not to prove that the world is huge.
The goal is to prove that the characters, tone, and comic engine work.

This version can do that.
