# The Heritage Center — Cast Archetypes v1

## Core Ensemble
Each main cast member represents a different style of bad authority and a different niche within the Center’s ecosystem.

---

## Chief Holly Bey — The Headmaster
### Function
The self-appointed founder and institutional center of gravity.

### Core traits
- speaks with total confidence
- cites nonexistent texts
- every challenge becomes proof of conspiracy
- thinks he founded the intellectual movement
- treats ownership of ideas like a form of royalty

### Public self-image
The visionary founder preserving buried truth.

### Private/confessional reality
He cares as much about legacy, ownership, and centrality as he does about “truth.”

### Comic contradiction
He talks like a prophet but behaves like a territorial administrator.

---

## Mr. Dense El — The Lecture Guru / Live General
### Function
The social enforcer and charisma bully.

### Core traits
- dominates every room
- always teaching
- interrupts everyone
- mixes charisma with misinformation
- runs half the Center through force of personality
- gives off mean-girl social energy in scholar form

### Public self-image
A fearless educator correcting people in real time.

### Private/confessional reality
He uses teaching as a vehicle for dominance, humiliation, and control.

### Comic contradiction
He presents himself as a truth teacher but mostly wants to win the room.

---

## Mild Indian — The Research Guy
### Function
The evidence hoarder whose research never fully lands.

### Core traits
- always has documents
- nothing is sourced properly
- loves screenshots, maps, weird charts, printouts, and fragments
- thinks volume of material equals proof
- never has a working phone
- hair is unkempt
- looks mildly hungover all the time

### Public self-image
The overworked archivist carrying the receipts for everyone else.

### Private/confessional reality
He is chaotic, disorganized, defensive, and only intermittently useful.

### Comic contradiction
He is forever “the documents guy,” yet rarely has a clean, usable document when needed.

---

## Olmec Donald — The Spiritual Historian
### Function
The metaphysical escape hatch for the whole institution.

### Core traits
- blends pseudo-history with mysticism
- everything becomes ancient prophecy
- impossible to pin down because all claims drift into metaphysical language
- sounds profound until the sentence is replayed
- visually memorable and serene in total chaos

### Public self-image
A keeper of deep ancestral memory and spiritual history.

### Private/confessional reality
Precision dissolves every time he is challenged; ambiguity is his protection.

### Comic contradiction
He presents intuition and vibration as a higher scholarly method.

### Visual note
Distinctive elder-poet / sacred-orator energy.

---

## Rootwords — The Young Convert
### Function
The unstable new blood trying hardest to belong.

### Core traits
- newest, loudest, most online
- parrots the school’s ideology with maximum certainty
- secretly understands the least
- desperate for status
- clips everything
- physically looks like he has been on a minor bender or has not slept properly in weeks

### Public self-image
A rising new teacher and next-generation truth voice.

### Private/confessional reality
He is recent, under-read, eager for validation, and constantly performing belief.

### Comic contradiction
He has maximum certainty with minimum foundation.

---

## A1 — Dean of Information / Respectability Scholar
### Function
The bureaucratic pseudo-legitimacy addict.

### Core traits
- tries to sound polished and academic
- uses big words incorrectly or too aggressively
- wants the school taken seriously
- spends all day frustrated by how unserious everyone else is
- is also fully pseudo, just cleaner and more institutionally minded about it
- obsessed with accreditation, paperwork, seals, titles, packets, and optics

### Public self-image
The man who can make the Heritage Center official.

### Private/confessional reality
He does not want truth; he wants organized legitimacy and institutional recognition.

### Comic contradiction
He mistakes polished nonsense for credible scholarship and believes bad ideas become curriculum if arranged correctly.

---

## Ensemble Dynamic Lock
This cast works because everyone fails differently:
- Chief Holly Bey = founder mythology / conspiracy authority
- Mr. Dense El = charisma domination / social control / live authority
- Mild Indian = chaotic evidence accumulation
- Olmec Donald = prophecy, vibration, metaphysical drift
- Rootwords = online convert energy / clip culture / status hunger
- A1 = bureaucratic pseudo-legitimacy / accreditation obsession

The show should keep these differences active in every group scene so conflict grows naturally from personality and niche.
